Understanding the Approval Routing and Capture Process

Approval routing and capture is based on the movement of approval sheets through the application. An approval sheet is the main mechanism of the approval process. It contains a list of approvers and the decisions each can make at approval time.

Defining Approval Decision Lists and Templates

Before beginning the approval process, you must define the applicable approval decisions and approval templates you will assign to the quality record to capture approvals. You define approval decision lists and approval templates in Modeling.

Assigning Approval Templates

You can assign approval templates for quality record resolution. When assigning approval templates for quality record resolution, you assign the templates to a category on an organization. The application then assigns the approval template to the quality record automatically based on the type of resolution option selected. You may also assign an approval template to a specific type of quality record, which only applies the approval to that record type.

Once an approval template has been assigned to a quality record it becomes an approval sheet. Approval sheets are then routed through the application.

Routing Approvals

The application routes an approval when the owner chooses to resolve a quality record and clicks the Route button. If an approval template was specified for the record type at the organization level, the owner can make modifications to that approval sheet before routing. Additionally, the owner may choose to select a different approval template, which can also be modified.

Generating Approver Concierge Tasks, Message Center Messages, and E-mail Notifications

When approvals are routed, the application generates an approval task in the Concierge and an approval message in the Message Center for all first level approvers identified on the approval template. The Concierge approval task is a link to perform the approval. The Message Center approval message includes an action to perform the approval.

Additionally, if a system administrator has configured an approval e-mail message, the application generates and sends an e-mail to all first level approvers. The e-mail message should tell approvers that they have an approval task.

Settings in the approval template determine whether only the specified approver or other users in the same role as the approver can approve the transition. If all members of a role can approve a resolution, the application does the following:

• Creates an approval task in the Concierge of the specified approver
• Generates a message and approval action in the Message Center of the specified approver
• Generates a message in the Message Center of appropriate role members
• Sends an e-mail to all role members if an approval e-mail has been configured.

When the first level approvers complete their approvals, the application automatically generates Concierge tasks, messages, and e-mail notifications for the next level of approvers identified on the approval template. The application also sends e-mail notifications to all users in the same role as those approvers, if the approval sheet and e-mails are set up appropriately.

Note: You define the approver e-mail distribution list and create the e-mail message in Modeling. Refer to the Opcenter EX MDD Modeling User Guide or the Opcenter EX CR Modeling User Guide for information.

Capturing Approval Decisions

If you are the specified approver, you click the Concierge or Message Center item indicating an approval task to view the quality record resolution you need to approve. If you are in the same role as the specified approver and have permission to perform the approval, you first access the record and then click the Approve action in the Quality Actions pane or from the Message Center to view the approval page. When you submit your approval decision, the application may require you to enter an electronic signature, if one has been configured for the approval process.

Canceling Approvals

Anyone with ownership rights can cancel the approval process regardless of whether any approvers have entered their decision. The status of the quality record must be In Review to cancel the approval process.

Viewing Approval History

You can view current and past approval cycles (based on routing date) for a particular approval template at any time by viewing the:

• Record's management page, specifically the Resolution tab
• Sign Approval page

The application captures and displays the following information in the cycle history for each approver:

• Level, role, name, approved by, and decision
• Approver comments, if applicable
• Routed date, due date
• Completion date (the date the approver approved or rejected the quality record)
Note: The application updates the Decision to Routing Canceled and leaves the Completion Date blank if the quality record owner cancels an approval.
