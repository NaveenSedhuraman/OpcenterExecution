Understanding Work Cells, Workstations, and Groups

Work Cells, Workstations, and groups are optional resource and resource group definitions that allow you to further refine your physical model. These optional definitions accommodate two important mechanisms:

• You can assign task lists to specific workstations at which processing occurs without requiring a Move transaction.
• You can track lot quantity movement across equipment at a significantly granular level.

Grouping Work Cells and Workstations

This diagram illustrates the relationships between work cells, workstations and their groups.

• A workstation divides a work cell into multiple stations.
• A work cell is always the parent to the workstations within it.
• A workstation should belong to one work cell only.
• The workstations in a workstation group should be assigned to different work cells.
Note: It is important to remember that the work cell and workstation setup is strictly optional, and it is your organization’s responsibility to make sure it accommodates your requirements.

Shop Floor Transactions

This table describes the behavior of shop floor transactions across work cells and workstations.

Transaction

Behavior

Split

The Split transaction is prohibited if the work cell or workstation requires container clearance. When you split a container that has partially completed tasks, the resulting containers have the same completed task lists.

Combine

Any or all of a container that is combined with another container takes on the completed tasks, work cell, and workstation attributes of the destination container.

Associate

When you associate containers, the completed tasks, work cell, and workstation of the from containers are cleared. Any completed tasks of the child containers are removed.

Disassociate

The Disassociate transaction is prohibited on a container if the work cell or workstation requires line clearance. If line clearance is not required, the child container that results in the disassociation retains a list of the parent container's completed tasks.

Hold

The Hold transaction does not impact line clearance at the work cell or workstation.

Release

The Release transaction does not impact the line clearance of the work cell or workstation.

Move

When you perform a Move transaction, the electronic procedure pertaining to the spec at the previous step is no longer available.
