Defining Sales Orders

A Sales Order is a request to provide a product. Sales orders typically originate from a customer, but can also originate from another factory or department.

A sales order defines a quantity of product expected by a customer by a date. This equates to a line item on a typical (ERP) sales order. A sales order is optionally assigned to a container when the container is started or updated. This provides the mechanism for relating WIP (containers) to customer orders.

A sales order can contain a list of the containers to which it is related. The optional relationship of a container to a sales order is established and maintained in the container's data record.

When Defining a Sales Order

Sales Order contains the optional modeling definitions Product and UOM.

Sales Order is an optional field in the transactions Start Two-Level, Container Maintenance, and Order Dispatch.

How to Define a Sales Order

Follow these steps to define a Sales Order:

1. Open the Sales Order page. The Sales Order page appears within the Modeling page.
2. Click New. Blank fields appear for you to define a new instance.
3. Enter a sales order name in the Sales Order field.
4. Enter optional information according to your business requirements. Refer to the field definitions table for information on the optional fields.
5. Click Save. The application saves the modeling object and displays a success message.
