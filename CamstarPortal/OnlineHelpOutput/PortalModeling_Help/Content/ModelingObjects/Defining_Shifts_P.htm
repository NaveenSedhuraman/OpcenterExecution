Defining Shifts

A Shift is a mechanism for grouping production information based on management responsibilities for workers that start and leave at a particular time. Shifts typically apply to manufacturing companies that have different groups of workers who start and end their days at different times. For example, the "Day" shift might work from 6:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m., and the "Night" shift might work from 6:00 p.m. to 6:00 a.m.

When Defining Shifts

Shift is an optional field in Mfg Calendar. However, a shift and team must be added to Mfg Calendar before an Excel spreadsheet can be imported.

Refer to "Common Fields on Modeling Pages" for information on the fields common to all modeling objects.

How to Define a Shift

Follow these steps to define a Shift:

1. Open the Shift page. The Shift page appears within the Modeling page.
2. Click New. Blank fields appear for you to define a new instance.
3. Enter a name for this shift in the Shift field.
4. Enter optional information according to your business requirements. Refer to "Common Fields on Modeling Pages" for information on the optional fields.
5. Click Save. The application saves the modeling object and displays a success message.
