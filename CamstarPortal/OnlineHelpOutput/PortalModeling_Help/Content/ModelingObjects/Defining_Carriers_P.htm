Defining Carriers

A Carrier is a physical entity like a vessel, vat, tray, drum, or a piece of equipment which is used to hold the material in your production processes. The most common example of a carrier is a tray. Carriers holding the materials are moved around on the shopfloor.

When Defining Carriers

You can assign a container to a carrier. Transactions are always performed on the container, not the carrier. You can view the container details and perform transactions on the container by providing the carrier name.

Carrier is an optional field in the Carrier Group modeling definition. You can assign a carrier to a carrier group to provide logical grouping. Carrier groups are used to provide selection lists of valid carriers and to validate user input.

Carrier contains the following optional Modeling definitions: Factory, Carrier Family, Vendor, Resource Type, and Resource Status Model.

Give each carrier a unique name. Carrier uniqueness extends to both resources and carriers. If you create a resource named “Tray-0015543,” then you cannot create a carrier named “Tray-0015543.”

Example of a carrier: An operator is making a drug in a factory and divides the 100 KG of the first drug ingredient into five trays which hold 20 KG each. The trays in this example are the carriers.

How to Define a Carrier

Follow these steps to define a Carrier:

1. Open the Carrier page. The Carrier page appears within the Modeling page.
2. Click New. Blank fields appear for you to define a new instance.
3. Enter a name for the carrier in the Carrier field.
4. Enter optional information according to your business requirements. Refer to the field definitions table for information on the optional fields.
5. Click Save. The application saves the modeling object and displays a success message.
