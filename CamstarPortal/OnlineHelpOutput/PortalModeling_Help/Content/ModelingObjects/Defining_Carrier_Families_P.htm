Defining Carrier Families

A Carrier Family provides a way to categorize carriers based on their attributes and according to your business requirements. You can then use the carrier family to set up maintenance schedules or use it as filter criteria for various shop floor transactions. Each carrier can have a carrier family, such as drum, vessel, tray, and so on.

When Defining a Carrier Family

These are examples of carrier families:

• 100 L Drum
• 200 L Drum
• Glass Tray
• Metallic Tray

Carrier Family is an optional field in the Carrier modeling object.

Refer to "Common Fields on Modeling Pages" for information on the fields common to all modeling objects.

How to Define a Carrier Family

Follow these steps to define a Carrier Family:

1. Open the Carrier Family page. The Carrier Family page appears within the Modeling page.
2. Click New. Blank fields appear for you to define a new instance.
3. Enter a name for the carrier family in the Carrier Family field.
4. Enter optional information according to your business requirements. Refer to "Common Fields on Modeling Pages" for information on the optional fields.
5. Click Save. The application saves the modeling object and displays a success message.
