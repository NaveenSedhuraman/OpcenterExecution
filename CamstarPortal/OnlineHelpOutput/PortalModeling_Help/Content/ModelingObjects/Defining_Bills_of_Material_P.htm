Defining Bills of Material (BOMs) and ERP BOMs

Manufactured products are built using an enumerated and well-defined list of raw materials and subassemblies. These are called material lists. A bill CDO facilitates a collection of material lists. There are two kinds of bill objects:

• Bill of Materials (BOM) - Defines the materials (components and quantities) needed to produce a specific product (end-item or subassembly). BOMs are referenced by a Product definition.
• Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) BOM - References steps in an ERP route instead of referencing steps in an Opcenter EX MDD or Opcenter EX CR workflow. It is a link between Opcenter EX MDD or Opcenter EX CR and ERP systems.

When Defining a BOM

BOM contains the optional modeling definitions Bill Type, Product, and Spec.

BOM is an optional field in the definition of Product.

Note: A BOM can have related WIP Messages. Refer to "Defining WIP Messages" and the Portal Shop Floor User Guide for information.

Defining Allowed Component Quantities on the BOM

You can define the required component quantity and the minimum and maximum allowed quantities using the Material List grid. You can also define component quantities using the Material List grid on the ERP BOM or manufacturing order. The material list defined for the BOM takes precedence over the material list defined for the ERP BOM. The material list defined for the manufacturing order and for the BOM take precedence over the material list defined for the ERP BOM. Refer to "Defining Allowed Component Quantities on the ERP BOM" and "Defining Allowed Component Quantities on the Manufacturing Order" for information.

Use the Minimum Allowed Quantity and the Maximum Allowed Quantity fields to define the allowed component quantities. These fields appear in the pop-ups displayed when you click the Add new row button to add a material to the Material List grid.

Phantom Bill Field

The Phantom Bill field on the BOM detail pop-up enables you to reference a phantom bill in your material list. A phantom bill is a logical grouping of materials that is listed as a single item on a material list. For example, a given product might include a particular component also produced by your enterprise. This component has its own bill of materials. Yet in the context of your main product, you want to include the component as a single item on the product's bill of materials. In this case, you would reference the item with a phantom bill of materials.

Referencing a phantom bill also has implications for the Get Requirements method. If a phantom bill does not have a Specification definition, it causes the Get Requirements logic to analyze components based on their assigned specifications.

Optional

Phantom Bill

Phantom bill is created for the purpose of grouping related materials when you do not want to list the items separately on the primary bill of materials. For example, when a certain group of materials is used for multiple finished products, you can create a phantom bill in order to reduce your maintenance efforts. The single material list item on your product's bill of materials acts as a pointer to the phantom bill of materials. If you enter a BOM on the Product page, it becomes the phantom bill for the product. If a BOM is not entered on the Product page, you can enter a phantom bill using the BOM Detail pop-up. If a BOM has been entered on the Product page and a phantom bill is selected on the BOM Detail page, then the phantom bill overrides the BOM entered on the Product page.

How to Define a BOM

Follow these steps to define a BOM:

1. Open the BOM page. The BOM page appears within the Modeling page.
2. Click New. Blank fields appear for you to define a new instance.
3. Enter the name of this BOM in the BOM field.
4. Enter the revision of this BOM in the Revision field.
5. Add items to the Material List grid by completing the "How to Add Items to the BOM Material List" procedure.
6. Enter optional information according to your business requirements. Refer to the field definitions table for information on the optional fields.
7. Click Save. The application saves the modeling object and displays a success message.

How to Add Items to the BOM Material List

Follow these steps to add an item to the BOM material list:

1. Perform the "How to Define a BOM" procedure.

Or

Select an existing BOM instance.

2. Expand the Materials section.
3. Click Add new row on the Material List grid. The Add pop-up appears.
4. Select a Product.
5. Select an Issue Control.
6. Enter a value in the Quantity Required field.
7. Enter optional information according to your business requirements. Refer to the field definitions table for information on the optional fields.
8. Click OK. The Add pop-up closes and the Material List Item appears in the Material List grid on the BOM page.
9. Repeat steps 3-8 to add additional items.
10. Click Save. The application displays a success message indicating the modeling object was updated.

When Defining an ERP BOM

ERP BOM contains:

• The required modeling definition Product (if you add a material list item).
• The optional modeling definitions ERP Route and UOM (if you add a material list item).

ERP BOM is an optional field in the definition of Product.

Note: An ERP BOM can have related WIP Messages. Refer to "Defining WIP Messages" and the Opcenter Execution Medical Device and Diagnostics Shop Floor User Guide or the Opcenter Execution Core Shop Floor User Guide for information.
