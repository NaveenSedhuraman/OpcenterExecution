Defining Bills of Process

A Bill of Process (BOP) allows you to modify the default behavior of the manufacturing process as defined by your Spec definitions.

A bill of process is a revisioned object, established using Opcenter EX MDD or Opcenter EX CR modeling. Once created, you assign the bill of process to a container, manufacturing order, or product using the Bill of Process Assignments list. When specified, a bill of process overrides the specification.

You can modify a bill of process once it is created, or view the details of the bill of process without making changes using this function.

When Defining a Bill of Process

When a bill of process is used at multiple spec steps, multiple times in one workflow, or across workflows, the override applies to all the steps in the spec.

You can use the bill of process to override the following fields on the spec:

• Document Set
• Electronic Procedure
• Electronic Signature Txn Map
• Label Txn Map
• Recipe File
• Resource Group
• Setup
• Training Req Group
• Transaction Map

All transactions except Modeling, Export and Import transactions check for the bill of process overrides.

A bill of process assigned to a parent container is assigned to all its children.

All resources you assign to a bill of process must exist and be up and available.

Any changes made to a bill of process once it is assigned are processed immediately as the container, manufacturing order, or product is processed.

If you delete a bill of process from a container, manufacturing order, or product, and assign a new bill of process in its place, the system records the BOP assignments to container, manufacturing order, and product in the Manufacturing and Modeling Audit Trails as applicable.

Associating a Bill of Process

Once you create a bill of process, it can be assigned to a container, manufacturing order, or product in any of the following ways:

• A container from the following pages:
• Container Start
• Container Maintenance
• Start - Two Level
• A manufacturing order from the Mfg Order page
• A product from the Product page

Bills of Process and Parent-Child Relationships

Special conditions apply to parent and child containers when a bill of process is assigned. Note the following:

• When executing a two-level or other multi-level start, the bill of process from the parent container is applied to all child containers.
• In the case of Associate and Disassociate transactions, the containers retain their individual bill of process.
• In the case of Split and Combine transactions, the split container is assigned the same bill of process as the split-from container; and the combined containers retain the bill of process from the combine-to container.
• In the case of Combine Quantity and Combine Container transactions (association of a container with another container) the bill of process associations with the containers are not altered.
• If a transaction is executed on a container, and a bill of process is assigned to the container, manufacturing order, or product, the overrides are applied. For example, during a Combine Container transaction, container C1 is the main container and container C2 is associated with container C1. Both have a bill of process associated with them. If you run a transaction on container C1, the bill of process related to container C1 is applied. When the containers are split, and other transactions are run on container C2, then the bill of process assigned to container C2 is applied.

Bills of Process and Electronic Procedures

Certain rules apply to bills of process when electronic procedures are involved. Note the following:

• Only object types referenced from the spec are supported.
• Override of individual task items (subentity) and task lists (revisioned object) is not supported.
• If you select a resource or work cell and/or a workstation on the line assignment screen, and there is an override specified on an electronic procedure of a spec that is currently executed but does not include the combination selected in the line assignment screen, the application displays an empty task list. No tasks are shown to execute.
• If you do not select a workstation or a work cell, and an override is defined for an Electronic Procedure that is currently executed, the application allows you to select workstations based on the allowed resources per the override in the electronic procedure. You can view the task list or task that is defined by the override.
• If you select a work cell at the time of move in, the Electronic Procedure task list task filter displays task lists or tasks only if workstations defined for the task are part of the work cell chosen at the time of move in.

EProcedures, Bills of Process and Multiple Containers

The following scenario describes the workflow of multiple containers when multiple bills of process and multiple electronic procedure requirements are involved:

1. Perform a Start transaction on container (C1) with bill of process BOP1 and electronic procedure requirement containing optional tasks.
2. Perform a Start transaction on a multi-level container (C2) with bill of process BOP2 and electronic procedure requirement containing required tasks.
3. Combine the child containers of C2 with container C1. BOP1 is applied.

Move the new container C1 (which now has the child containers) to the next step using the Operational View page. The container is successfully moved to the next step even though the electronic procedure requirement tasks were required. In this case, the required tasks tied to the child container’s bill of process electronic procedure requirement are ignored because the primary container’s bill of process is applied.

How to Define a Bill of Process

Follow these steps to define a Bill of Process (BOP):

1. Open the Bill Of Process page. The Bill Of Process page appears within the Modeling page.
2. Click New. Blank fields appear for you to define a new instance.
3. Enter the name of the bill of process in the Bill of Process field.
4. Enter the revision of this bill of process in the Revision field.
5. Select the Revision of Record check box if this is the revision of record.
6. Enter optional information according to your business requirements. Refer to the field definitions table for information on the optional fields.
7. Click Save. The application saves the modeling object and displays a success message.

How to Add a Bill of Process Override

Follow these steps to add a bill of process override:

1. Perform the "How to Define a Bill of Process" procedure.

Or

Select an existing Bill of Process instance.

2. Expand the Override section.
3. Click Add new row on the Spec Override grid. The Add pop-up appears.
4. Enter or select a Spec.
5. Specify the fields on the selected spec that are to be overridden.
6. Click OK. The bill of process is updated.
7. Repeat steps 3-6 to add another override to the bill of process.
8. Click Save. The application displays a success message indicating the modeling object was updated.
