Electronic Procedures and Process Computations

Electronic procedures and process computations offer a method of viewing and tracking groups of production tasks. An electronic procedure is a revisionable object that enables you to assign a collection of tasks to a specification (spec). A task is a unit of work required to be completed on a container at a specification. Process computation tasks allow you to incorporate user-defined constants and detailed computation expressions when defining your tasks.

An electronic procedure provides an electronic record for each task. The ability to assign an electronic procedure to a spec allows you to perform multiple steps to complete work on the spec without having to break each task into a separate spec, which would necessitate many more Move transactions.

As you define your model for electronic procedures, you logically group tasks into task lists. You also establish whether each task is required or optional. Each of your electronic procedures may contain one or more task lists.
