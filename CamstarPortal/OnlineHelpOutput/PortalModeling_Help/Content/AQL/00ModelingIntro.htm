AQL Sampling

Sampling inspection is a statistical practice in which a subset of a container or a lot (group of containers) is tested to estimate the characteristics of the whole lot to prevent producing off-quality product. The Acceptable Quality Limits (AQL) Sampling module enables you to model and execute sampling plans for manufacturing containers. Sampling plans define the number of samples and frequency of sampling required and the associated allowable rejects. Based on the collected sampling data, AQL Sampling determines if a container or lot is accepted or rejected. A container or lot fails sampling if the number of rejects exceeds the acceptable reject count.

You can model AQL Sampling using modeling objects which are unique to AQL Sampling.
