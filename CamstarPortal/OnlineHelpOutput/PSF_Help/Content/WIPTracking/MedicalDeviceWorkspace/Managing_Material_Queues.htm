Managing Material Queues

You can use the Manage Material Queue or Manage Material Queue Simple modeling object to load or unload containers, lots, or products onto a material queue from an inventory location. You can load multiple containers, lots, and products onto a material queue. You can also assign a resource to the material queue and indicate the material queue is the current active material queue for consumption of component products.

When Managing Material Queues

The Manage Material Queue page displays a list of materials required to produce the selected product or manufacturing order. The list assists the user in determining what is required to be loaded onto the material queue. The application displays the material requirements for the selected product or manufacturing order in the Material Requirements grid of the Manage Material Queue page. The Manage Material Queue Simple page does not show the list of materials required and is useful for just scanning the item and assigning it to the material queue.

You can validate that the material you are loading onto the material queue is valid for the product or manufacturing order you are going to produce. Select the product or manufacturing order before loading the material to validate. If you want to enforce validation when loading items onto the material queue, you can select the Validate Queue check box on the Resource modeling object. Refer to the Opcenter Execution Medical Device and Diagnostics Modeling User Guide or the Opcenter Execution Core Modeling User Guide for information.

When loading materials onto the material queue, the type of items you load determines the type of consumption that occurs according to these rules:

• Containers satisfy serialized or bulk issue types.
• Lots satisfy Lot and Stock issue types.
• Products satisfy Qty Only issue types.

Sequencing the Order of Material Consumption

Materials are consumed in the order they appear in the Material Queue Details grid. You can use the up and down arrows to sequence the order of consumption for the materials. This is useful when you have containers or lots of the same material loaded onto the material queue and you want to sequence them in the order they should be consumed. For example, you load 10 units of Product A with an expiration date of February 18 onto the material queue. Then, you load another 10 units of Product A with an expiration date of January 18. You can use the up arrow to sequence the units expiring in January ahead of the units expiring in February.

How to Load Material onto a Material Queue

Follow these steps to load material onto a material queue:

1. Open the Manage Material Queue page. The Manage Material Queue page appears.

Or

Open the Manage Material Queue Simple page. The Manage Material Queue Simple page appears.

2. Do you want to load material according to a material queue or a resource?

If you want to load material according to . . .

Then . . .

Material Queue
a. Select a Material Queue from the list. The application populates the Resource field with the associated resource, if applicable. The application selects the Is Active check box if the material queue is active. The application populates the Material Queue Details grid with the containers, lots, or products currently loaded onto the material queue. The application enables the Submit and Reset buttons.
b. Go to step 3.

Resource
a. Select a Resource from the list. The application populates the Material Queue field and selects the Is Active check box if there is an active material queue associated with the resource. The application populates the Material Queue Details grid with the containers, lots, or products currently loaded onto the material queue. The application enables the Submit and Reset buttons.
b. Go to step 3.
3. Do you want to validate material requirements according to a manufacturing order or a product?

If you want to load

material according to. . .

Then . . .

Manufacturing Order
a. Select a Mfg Order from the list. The application disables the Product field. The Route Step field appears on the Manage Material Queue page.
b. Optionally, select a Route Step from the list if you want to validate only according to material requirements at that specific route step.
c. Go to step 4.

Product
a. Select a Product from the list. The application disables the Mfg Order field. The Spec field appears on the Manage Material Queue page if the Product has a BOM.
b. Optionally, select a Spec from the list if you want to validate only according to material requirements at that specific spec.
c. Go to step 4.
4. Do you want to search for containers, lots, or products associated with an inventory location?

If . . .

Then . . .

Yes
a. Select an Inventory Location from the list.
b. Click Search. The Inventory Details pop-up appears with Inventory Details grid populated with containers, lots, or products assigned to the inventory location.
c. Select a row in the Inventory Details grid.
d. Click OK. The Inventory Details pop-up closes. The application populates the Container or Lot, Product, and Qty fields.
e. Go to step 5.

No
a. Select an Inventory Location from the list.
b. Select a Container or Lot or Product from the list. The application populates the Product and Qty information if you select a container or lot.
c. Go to step 5.
5. Click Load. The application adds the container, lot, or product to the Material Queue Details grid. The application also updates the Material Queue modeling instance.

How to Unload Material from a Material Queue

Follow these steps to unload material from a material queue:

1. Open the Manage Material Queue page. The Manage Material Queue page appears.

Or

Open the Manage Material Queue Simple page. The Manage Material Queue Simple page appears.

2. Do you want to unload material according to a material queue or a resource?

If you want to unload material according to . . .

Then . . .

Material Queue
a. Select a Material Queue from the list. The application populates the Resource field with the associated resource, if applicable. The application selects the Is Active check box if the material queue is active. The application populates the Material Queue Details grid with the containers, lots, or products currently loaded onto the material queue. The application enables the Submit and Reset buttons.
b. Go to step 3.

Resource
a. Select a Resource from the list. The application populates the Material Queue field and selects the Is Active check box if there is an active material queue associated with the resource. The application populates the Material Queue Details grid with the containers, lots, or products currently loaded onto the material queue. The application enables the Submit and Reset buttons.
b. Go to step 3.
3. Select one or more rows in the Material Queue Details grid. The application enables the Unload button.
4. Click Unload. The application removes the container, lot, or product from the Material Queue Details grid. The application also updates the Material Queue modeling instance and returns the material to the Inventory Location it was loaded from.

How to Load Materials to a Material Queue Kit

Follow these steps to load materials to a material queue kit:

1. Open the Manage Material Queue page. The Manage Material Queue page appears.

Or

Open the Manage Material Queue Simple page. The Manage Material Queue Simple page appears.

2. Select a Material Queue from the list. The application selects the Is Active check box if the material queue is active. The application populates the Product, Container, Mfg Order, or Operation for the selected material queue. The application enables the Submit and Reset buttons.
3. Do you want to search for containers or lots associated with an inventory location?

If . . .

Then . . .

Yes
a. Select an Inventory Location from the list.
b. Click Search. The Inventory Details pop-up appears with the Inventory Details grid populated with containers or lots assigned to the inventory location.
c. Select a row in the Inventory Details grid.
d. Click OK. The Inventory Details pop-up closes. The application populates the Container or Lot, Product, and Qty fields.
e. Go to step 4.

No
a. Select an Inventory Location from the list.
b. Select a Container or Lot or Product from the list. The application populates the Product and Qty information if you selected a Container or Lot.
c. Go to step 4.
4. Do you want to validate material requirements according to a manufacturing order or a product?

If . . .

Then . . .

Manufacturing
Order
a. Select the Kitting Order check box. The application populates the Mfg Order field with orders desingated as kitting orders.
b. Select a Mfg Order from the list. The application disables the Product field. The Route Step field appears on the Manage Material Queue page. The application populates the Material Queue grid with the containers or lots currently in the material queue.
c. Optionally, select a Route Step from the list if you want to validate only according to material requirements at that specific route step.
d. Go to step 5.

Product
a. Select a Product from the list. The application disables the Mfg Order field. The Spec field appears on the Manage Material Queue page if the Product has a BOM.
b. Optionally, select a Spec from the list if you want to validate only according to material requirements at that specific spec.
c. Go to step 5.
5. Click Submit. The application adds the container or lot to the Material Queue Details grid representing the kit. The application also updates the Material Queue modeling instance. A message is sent to an ERP if the Send to ERP is selected and an ERP is configured .

How to Unload Materials from a Material Queue Kit

Follow these steps to unload materials from a material queue kit:

1. Open the Manage Material Queue page. The Manage Material Queue page appears.

Or

Open the Manage Material Queue Simple page. The Manage Material Queue Simple page appears.

2. Select a Material Queue from the list. The application populates the Resource field with the associated resource if applicable. The application selects the Is Active check box if the material queue is active. The application populates the Material Queue grid with the containers or lots currently in the material queue. The application enables the Submit and Reset buttons.
3. Select one or more rows in the Material Queue Details grid. The application enables the Unload button.
4. Click Unload. The application removes the materials from the Material Queue Details grid. The application also updates the Material Queue modeling instance and returns the material to the Inventory Location it was kitted from.
